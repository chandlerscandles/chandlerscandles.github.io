Candle Care Instructions

Thank you so much for buying a candle from me! I really want you to relax and enjoy your candle, so I've outlined some guidance to ensure you can burn your candle safely and also get the most out of it and prolong its lifespan:

Preparation

  • Trim the Wick: Before lighting, trim the wick to about 1/4 inch (6 mm) to prevent uneven burning, soot, and a large flame. If you're used to other candles note that beeswax candles may need the wick trimmed more frequently during the burn.
  • Remove Debris: Ensure the candle is free of any debris, such as matches or wick trimmings, which can catch fire.
  • Container Choice: If you'd like to use a container for your beeswax candle, ensure the container can handle the higher heat produced by burning beeswax. Glass, ceramic, or metal containers are typically suitable.

Placement

  • Stable Surface: Place the candle on a stable, heat-resistant surface to prevent tipping and protect surfaces from heat damage. A slanted surface that is not completely flat can cause the molten wax to pool on one side of the candle and can cause an uneven burn. You can check if your burning surface is level by using a Spirit Level or a smartphone app such as the iPhone Measure app.
  • Away from Flammables: Keep the candle away from flammable materials like curtains, paper, or anything that can catch fire easily.
  • Ventilation: Ensure the room is well-ventilated, but ensure you avoid drafts, vents, or air currents which can cause uneven burning or flare-ups.

While Burning

  • Initial Burn: Allow the candle to burn long enough to create a melt pool that the entire top layer of wax has melted to the edges. This can take 2-4 hours. This prevents tunneling and ensures an even burn. Note that the first burn is particularly important for beeswax candles.
  • Do Not Burn for Too Long: Avoid burning the candle for more than 4 hours at a time. Long burns can cause the wick to mushroom, leading to soot and a larger flame.
  • Never Leave Unattended: Always keep a burning candle within sight and never leave it unattended, especially around children and pets.
  • Use a Snuffer: Use a candle snuffer to extinguish the candle. Blowing it out can cause hot wax to splatter and produce smoke.
  • Keep Away from Children and Pets: Ensure that candles are out of reach of children and pets to prevent accidents.

Post-Burning

  • Check Wick Position: Ensure the wick is centered and upright after extinguishing. Recenter the wick if necessary to avoid uneven burning next time. Beeswax tends to be more viscous than other waxes. Re-center the wick after extinguishing the candle, while the wax is still soft.
  • Allow to Cool: Let the candle cool completely before relighting, trimming the wick, or moving it. Beeswax candles tend to burn hotter and longer. Allow ample cooling time before relighting.
  • Store Properly: Store candles in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to prevent discoloration and warping. Beeswax may develop a white, powdery coating known as "bloom". This is a natural occurrence and can be gently polished off with a soft cloth if desired

Additional Safety Tips

  • Use Candle Holders: Use appropriate candle holders that are designed to catch any drips and withstand the heat.
  • Discard Safely: Discontinue use when about 1/2 inch (12 mm) of wax remains in the container to prevent heat damage to the surface and container.
